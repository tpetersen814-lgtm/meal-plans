Meal 1: Falafel-Spiced Veggie Patties

Serves 4–5 | Active time: ~20 min | Total: ~35 min | Great for make-ahead
▼

Crispy pan-fried chickpea patties packed with cumin, coriander, and fresh herbs, served in warm pitas with a cool yogurt sauce and crunchy vegetables. The mixture can be made the night before, making this a genuine 20-minute weeknight dinner.

Ingredients

For the patties:

  • 2 cans (15 oz each) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 small onion, roughly chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • ½ cup fresh parsley (or cilantro), packed
  • 4 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1½ teaspoons cumin
  • 1 teaspoon coriander
  • ½ teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ¾ teaspoon salt
  • ¼ teaspoon black pepper
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil or vegetable oil (for pan-frying)

For the yogurt sauce:

  • 1 cup plain whole milk yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons cucumber, finely minced
  • Pinch of salt

To serve:

  • 4–5 pitas (8-inch)
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • ½ small red onion, thinly sliced

Instructions

  1. Make the yogurt sauce: Stir together yogurt, lemon juice, minced garlic, minced cucumber, and a pinch of salt. Refrigerate until serving — it gets better as it sits.
  2. Add chickpeas, onion, garlic, and parsley to a food processor. Pulse 10–15 times until finely chopped and crumbly — like coarse crumbs, not a smooth paste.
  3. Transfer to a bowl. Add flour and all spices. Stir to combine. Should hold together when pressed. If too wet, add another tablespoon of flour. Refrigerate 10 minutes if you have time.
  4. Using slightly damp hands, shape about 2 tablespoons of mixture per patty into a round disc, about ½ inch thick. You should get 12–16 patties.
  5. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Cook patties in batches — 3–4 minutes per side until deep golden brown. Don’t move them; let a crust form before flipping.
  6. Warm pitas in a dry skillet 30 seconds per side. Spread yogurt sauce inside, tuck in 3–4 patties, and add cucumber, tomatoes, and red onion. Serve immediately.
🕐 Make-Ahead Options:
  • Night before: Make the yogurt sauce and the patty mixture — both keep in the fridge. The mixture firms up overnight and becomes easier to shape.
  • Up to 24 hours ahead: Shape uncooked patties on a parchment-lined plate, cover, and refrigerate. Day-of: just fry and serve.
  • Veggie prep: Slice cucumber, halve tomatoes, slice red onion. Store in separate containers.
😬 For baby (12 months+): Serve 1–2 patties cut into small pieces — crispy outside, soft inside. Yogurt sauce on the side for dipping practice. Soft pita strips for self-feeding. Skip the raw red onion.
Storage & Freezer: Cooked patties keep 4 days in the fridge. Reheat in a dry skillet 2 min per side. Freeze uncooked shaped patties; cook from frozen adding 2 extra minutes per side. Up to 3 months.
💡 No food processor? Mash chickpeas with a fork and finely mince everything by hand. Chunkier texture, just as tasty.

Meal 2: Mujadara (Lebanese Lentils & Rice with Crispy Onions)

Serves 4–5 | Active time: ~15 min | Total: ~45 min | Almost entirely hands-off
▼

One of the great pantry meals of the world. Earthy lentils and rice with warming spices, crowned with deeply caramelized crispy onions. Humble, filling, and surprisingly addictive. A staple across Lebanon, Syria, and Egypt — and one of the most affordable dinners you can make.

Ingredients

For the mujadara:

  • 1 cup green or brown lentils, rinsed
  • ¾ cup long-grain white rice or basmati, rinsed
  • 3½ cups water or vegetable broth (divided)
  • 2 large onions, halved and thinly sliced into half-moons
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 1½ teaspoons cumin
  • ½ teaspoon coriander
  • ½ teaspoon cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon allspice (optional but traditional)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ¼ teaspoon black pepper

To serve:

  • 1 cup plain whole milk yogurt
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 2 medium tomatoes, diced (or 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions

  1. Combine lentils and 2 cups water in a pot. Bring to a boil, reduce heat, and simmer uncovered for 15 minutes until partially cooked but still firm. Drain and set aside.
  2. While lentils simmer, heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a large wide pot over medium-high heat. Add sliced onions and cook, stirring every few minutes, for 20–25 minutes until deep golden brown and jammy. Reserve a handful of the crispiest onions on a paper towel for topping.
  3. Add all spices to the remaining onions. Stir and cook 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Add par-cooked lentils, rinsed rice, and 1½ cups water or broth. Stir to combine. Bring to a boil, reduce to lowest setting, cover tightly, and cook 20 minutes. Do not lift the lid.
  5. Remove from heat. Keep covered and let steam 10 minutes. While it rests, toss diced cucumber and tomatoes with lemon juice, a drizzle of olive oil, and a pinch of salt.
  6. Fluff gently with a fork. Taste and adjust salt. Serve topped with crispy onions alongside yogurt and the cucumber-tomato salad.
🕐 Make-Ahead Options:
  • Night before: Caramelize the onions — keeps in the fridge up to 5 days. Day-of cooking is entirely hands-off.
  • Full dish: Make up to 3 days ahead. Reheat with a splash of water. Flavors deepen overnight.
  • Salad: Dice the veg the night before; dress just before serving.
😬 For baby (12 months+): Wonderful baby food — soft, slightly sticky texture easy to eat from a tray. Mild spices great for palate development. Skip the crispy onion topping. High in iron, protein, and fiber.
Storage & Freezer: Keeps 4–5 days in the fridge; gets better on day 2. Add a splash of water when reheating. Freezes well up to 3 months. Store crispy onions separately at room temp.
⚡ The caramelized onions are the key: Don’t shortcut them. 20–25 minutes of patient cooking is what makes this extraordinary. Put on a podcast and let them do their thing.
