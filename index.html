Meal 1: Spinach & Feta Quesadillas with Fried Egg

Serves 4–5 | Prep: 5 min | Total: ~20 min | Great way to use up leftover feta
▼

Crispy flour tortillas filled with wilted spinach, crumbled feta, and a fried egg — fast, satisfying, and a great use of leftover feta. Serve with sour cream, salsa, or a simple side salad. These come together one or two at a time in a skillet, so it's a quick assembly-line dinner.

Ingredients

For the quesadillas:

  • 8–10 flour tortillas (8-inch)
  • 4–5 oz leftover feta cheese, crumbled
  • 4 cups baby spinach
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella or Monterey Jack (helps the quesadilla hold together)
  • 4–5 eggs
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon butter (for frying eggs and quesadillas)
  • Salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes (optional)

To serve:

  • Sour cream
  • Salsa or hot sauce
  • Avocado, sliced (optional)

Instructions

  1. Wilt the spinach: heat olive oil in a wide skillet over medium heat. Add garlic and cook 30 seconds. Add spinach and toss for 1–2 minutes until just wilted. Season with salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes if using. Transfer to a bowl and squeeze out any excess liquid — wet spinach will make soggy quesadillas.
  2. Fry the eggs: in the same skillet over medium-low heat, add a small knob of butter and fry the eggs to your liking — sunny side up or over easy both work well. Season with salt and pepper. Set aside.
  3. Assemble quesadillas: lay a tortilla flat. On one half, scatter a small handful of shredded mozzarella, a spoonful of wilted spinach, a crumble of feta, and place a fried egg on top. Fold the empty half over to close.
  4. Cook quesadillas: heat the skillet over medium heat and add a small amount of butter. Cook each folded quesadilla for 2–3 minutes per side, pressing gently with a spatula, until golden and crispy and the cheese is melted. Work in batches.
  5. Cut each in half and serve immediately with sour cream, salsa, and avocado.
🕐 Timing tip: Wilt all the spinach first in one go, then fry the eggs all at once, then assemble and cook quesadillas in batches. The whole thing is genuinely 20 minutes. Spinach can also be wilted the night before and stored in the fridge.
🍼 For baby (12 months+): Quesadillas are perfect for baby! Cut into small pieces or strips — the soft tortilla, melted cheese, and egg are all great textures at this age. Feta is fine in moderate amounts. Offer a few pieces of plain wilted spinach on the tray as well. Skip the salsa and hot sauce for baby; a small spoonful of plain sour cream or avocado is great on the side.
Storage: Best eaten fresh — quesadillas go soft in the fridge. Leftover wilted spinach filling keeps 3 days and can be used in eggs, pasta, or on toast. If you do refrigerate cooked quesadillas, reheat in a dry skillet to crisp them back up rather than microwaving.
💡 Feta tip: Feta doesn't melt, so the mozzarella is what holds the quesadilla together — don't skip it. The feta just adds that salty, tangy hit of flavour. A little goes a long way, which makes this a great leftover-feta recipe.

Meal 2: Chana Masala

Serves 4–5 | Prep: 10 min | Total: ~35 min | Serve with rice or naan
▼

A deeply spiced, tangy chickpea curry that's one of the great weeknight pantry meals. One onion to chop is the main prep work — after that, canned chickpeas and tomatoes do all the heavy lifting while it simmers. Tastes like it took much longer than it did. Serve with basmati rice or naan, with yogurt on the side to cool things down.

Ingredients

For the chana masala:

  • 2 cans (15 oz each) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 medium onion, finely diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, minced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • ½ cup water
  • 1½ teaspoons garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon coriander
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric
  • ½ teaspoon chili powder (reduce or omit for a milder version)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, to serve

To serve:

  • 2 cups dry basmati rice, cooked
  • Naan bread (optional)
  • Plain yogurt

Instructions

  1. Start the rice cooking according to package directions — it'll be ready when the curry is done.
  2. Heat oil in a large pot or skillet over medium heat. Add onion and cook 6–7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until soft and golden.
  3. Add garlic and ginger. Cook 1–2 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Add tomato paste and stir, cooking 1 minute. Add garam masala, cumin, coriander, smoked paprika, turmeric, and chili powder. Stir and cook 1 minute — the spices will bloom and become very fragrant.
  5. Add diced tomatoes (with their liquid), water, and sugar. Stir everything together, scraping any bits from the bottom of the pan. Bring to a simmer and cook 5 minutes until the sauce starts to thicken.
  6. Add chickpeas and stir to coat. Simmer uncovered for 12–15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce is thick and the chickpeas have absorbed the flavours. Use the back of a spoon to gently mash a few chickpeas against the side of the pot — this thickens the sauce nicely.
  7. Stir in lemon juice. Taste and season generously with salt. The flavour should be bold, tangy, and warming.
  8. Serve over rice or with naan, topped with fresh cilantro and a dollop of plain yogurt.
🕐 Make-Ahead: Chana masala is one of those dishes that's noticeably better the next day as the spices deepen. Make the full batch ahead and reheat gently with a splash of water. The sauce keeps 5 days in the fridge and freezes beautifully for up to 3 months — freeze in individual portions for easy weeknight reheats.
🍼 For baby (12 months+): Chickpeas are excellent for baby — high in iron, protein, and fibre. Scoop out some chickpeas before adding the chili powder for a milder portion, or serve the full dish if baby is used to spices (the spice level is warm but not hot). Lightly mash a few chickpeas to a texture baby can manage. Serve with soft rice. Plain yogurt on the side is a great cooling accompaniment and adds calcium.
Storage & Freezer: Keeps 5 days in the fridge; gets better on days 2 and 3. Freezes well for up to 3 months. Reheat over low heat with a splash of water, stirring occasionally. Store rice separately.
⚡ The one prep job: Dicing the onion is the main active work here — about 3–4 minutes. After that the whole dish is stirring and simmering. Start the rice at the same time as the onion and everything is ready together.

Meal 3: Spaghetti with Hidden Veggie Sauce

Serves 4–5 | Prep: 10 min | Total: ~35 min | Adapted from The Modern Nonna
▼

Your favourite jarred marinara sauce upgraded with diced carrots and zucchini — sautéed until completely soft, then blended right into the sauce so they disappear entirely. The vegetables add natural sweetness, body, and hidden nutrition. A great trick for getting extra veg into the whole family without anyone noticing. Adapted from The Modern Nonna's hidden veggie pasta sauce concept.

Ingredients

For the sauce:

  • 1 jar (24–25 oz) favourite marinara or pasta sauce
  • 2 medium carrots, diced
  • 1 medium zucchini, diced
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • ½ teaspoon sugar (balances the acidity)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Parmesan, grated, to serve

For the pasta:

  • 1 lb (16 oz) spaghetti
  • Salt for pasta water

Instructions

  1. Dice the carrots and zucchini into roughly ½-inch pieces — they don't need to be precise since they're getting blended. This is the main prep, about 5 minutes.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet or pot over medium heat. Add onion and cook 4–5 minutes until soft and translucent. Add garlic and cook 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the diced carrots. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 5–6 minutes until starting to soften. Add the diced zucchini, oregano, salt, and pepper. Cook another 4–5 minutes until both vegetables are completely soft and tender — you want them fully cooked through so they blend smoothly.
  4. Transfer the sautéed vegetables to a blender. Add about ½ cup of the jarred marinara sauce to help it blend. Blend until completely smooth — hold the lid down with a folded tea towel in case of steam. Alternatively use an immersion blender directly in the pot.
  5. Return the blended veg mixture to the pan. Pour in the remaining jarred sauce and add sugar. Stir to combine — the sauce will be thick, smooth, and a beautiful deep orange-red. Bring to a gentle simmer and cook 8–10 minutes, stirring occasionally. Taste and adjust seasoning.
  6. While the sauce simmers, bring a large pot of well-salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti according to package directions until al dente. Reserve ½ cup pasta water before draining.
  7. Add the drained spaghetti to the sauce. Toss well to coat, adding a splash of pasta water to loosen if needed. Serve topped with plenty of grated parmesan.
🕐 Make-Ahead: The sauce (without pasta) is excellent made ahead — keeps 5 days in the fridge and freezes well for up to 3 months. Make a big batch and freeze half in a freezer bag for a future zero-effort dinner. Cook the pasta fresh when serving.
🍼 For baby (12 months+): This sauce is genuinely ideal baby food — the vegetables are completely undetectable in texture and the sauce is mild and nutritious. Serve with pasta cut to an appropriate size, or offer short pasta shapes like penne or rigatoni which are easier for small hands to pick up. A little grated parmesan on top adds calcium. This is a great meal to batch and freeze in baby-sized portions.
Storage & Freezer: Sauce keeps 5 days in the fridge, 3 months in the freezer. Store separately from cooked pasta. Reheat sauce in a pan with a splash of water, then cook fresh pasta. Dressed pasta keeps 3 days but sauce thickens — add water when reheating.
💡 The prep: Dicing the carrots and zucchini is the main job — about 5 minutes. No grating, no squeezing. Make sure the veg is fully soft before blending or you'll get a gritty sauce. Hold the blender lid down with a folded tea towel when blending hot liquid.

👶 Baby Breakfast

Banana Oat Pancakes

Baby Breakfast | Makes ~16–20 small pancakes | Prep: 5 min | Cook: 15 min
▼

Simple 4-ingredient pancakes perfect for little ones. Naturally sweet, soft, and easy for small hands to grab. Freezes beautifully — make a big batch on the weekend for quick daycare breakfasts all week.

Ingredients

  • 2 ripe bananas
  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 2 eggs
  • ½ teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon butter or oil (for the pan)

Instructions

  1. Add bananas (broken into chunks), oats, eggs, and cinnamon to a blender or food processor. Blend until smooth, about 20–30 seconds. The batter will be thinner than traditional pancake batter — that's normal. Let it rest 5 minutes so the oats absorb liquid and the batter thickens slightly.
  2. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium-low heat. Add butter or oil and spread around. Medium-low is important — these brown faster than regular pancakes because of the banana sugars.
  3. Pour about 1 tablespoon of batter per pancake (silver-dollar size). Cook until bubbles form on the surface and edges look set, about 2 minutes.
  4. Gently flip each pancake and cook 1–2 more minutes until golden on the bottom. Use a thin spatula — these are delicate. Transfer to a plate or cooling rack.
  5. Cool completely before storing. For daycare: pack 3–4 in a small container. Great cold or microwaved for 20–30 seconds.
🍼 For baby (12 months+): These were designed for baby! Serve at room temperature — small enough to grab, soft enough to gum. Add 1 tbsp peanut butter to the blender for extra protein and healthy fat. Also great with a small smear of yogurt on top.
Freezer Instructions: Cool completely, lay in a single layer on a parchment-lined sheet and freeze 1–2 hours, then transfer to a freezer bag. Keeps up to 3 months. Microwave straight from frozen for 30–45 seconds, or pull out the night before.
💡 Variations: Toss a small handful of spinach into the blender — it disappears in the batter and adds iron. For adults: top with yogurt and fresh berries.
