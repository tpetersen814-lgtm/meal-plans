Meal 1: Falafel-Spiced Veggie Patties

Serves 4–5 | Active time: ~20 min | Total: ~35 min | Great for make-ahead
▼

Crispy pan-fried chickpea patties packed with cumin, coriander, and fresh herbs, served in warm pitas with a cool yogurt sauce and crunchy vegetables. The mixture can be made the night before, making this a genuine 20-minute weeknight dinner.

Ingredients

For the patties:

  • 2 cans (15 oz each) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 small onion, roughly chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • ½ cup fresh parsley (or cilantro), packed
  • 4 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1½ teaspoons cumin
  • 1 teaspoon coriander
  • ½ teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ¾ teaspoon salt
  • ¼ teaspoon black pepper
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil or vegetable oil (for pan-frying)

For the yogurt sauce:

  • 1 cup plain whole milk yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons cucumber, finely minced
  • Pinch of salt

To serve:

  • 4–5 pitas (8-inch)
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • ½ small red onion, thinly sliced

Instructions

  1. Make the yogurt sauce: Stir together yogurt, lemon juice, minced garlic, minced cucumber, and a pinch of salt. Refrigerate until serving — it gets better as it sits.
  2. Add chickpeas, onion, garlic, and parsley to a food processor. Pulse 10–15 times until finely chopped and crumbly — like coarse crumbs, not a smooth paste.
  3. Transfer to a bowl. Add flour and all spices. Stir to combine. Should hold together when pressed. If too wet, add another tablespoon of flour. Refrigerate 10 minutes if you have time.
  4. Using slightly damp hands, shape about 2 tablespoons of mixture per patty into a round disc, about ½ inch thick. You should get 12–16 patties.
  5. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Cook patties in batches — 3–4 minutes per side until deep golden brown. Don’t move them; let a crust form before flipping.
  6. Warm pitas in a dry skillet 30 seconds per side. Spread yogurt sauce inside, tuck in 3–4 patties, and add cucumber, tomatoes, and red onion. Serve immediately.
🕐 Make-Ahead Options:
  • Night before: Make the yogurt sauce and the patty mixture — both keep in the fridge. The mixture firms up overnight and becomes easier to shape.
  • Up to 24 hours ahead: Shape uncooked patties on a parchment-lined plate, cover, and refrigerate. Day-of: just fry and serve.
  • Veggie prep: Slice cucumber, halve tomatoes, slice red onion. Store in separate containers.
😬 For baby (12 months+): Serve 1–2 patties cut into small pieces — crispy outside, soft inside. Yogurt sauce on the side for dipping practice. Soft pita strips for self-feeding. Skip the raw red onion.
Storage & Freezer: Cooked patties keep 4 days in the fridge. Reheat in a dry skillet 2 min per side. Freeze uncooked shaped patties; cook from frozen adding 2 extra minutes per side. Up to 3 months.
💡 No food processor? Mash chickpeas with a fork and finely mince everything by hand. Chunkier texture, just as tasty.

Meal 2: Mujadara (Lebanese Lentils & Rice with Crispy Onions)

Serves 4–5 | Active time: ~15 min | Total: ~45 min | Almost entirely hands-off
▼

One of the great pantry meals of the world. Earthy lentils and rice with warming spices, crowned with deeply caramelized crispy onions. Humble, filling, and surprisingly addictive. A staple across Lebanon, Syria, and Egypt — and one of the most affordable dinners you can make.

Ingredients

For the mujadara:

  • 1 cup green or brown lentils, rinsed
  • ¾ cup long-grain white rice or basmati, rinsed
  • 3½ cups water or vegetable broth (divided)
  • 2 large onions, halved and thinly sliced into half-moons
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 1½ teaspoons cumin
  • ½ teaspoon coriander
  • ½ teaspoon cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon allspice (optional but traditional)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ¼ teaspoon black pepper

To serve:

  • 1 cup plain whole milk yogurt
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 2 medium tomatoes, diced (or 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions

  1. Combine lentils and 2 cups water in a pot. Bring to a boil, reduce heat, and simmer uncovered for 15 minutes until partially cooked but still firm. Drain and set aside.
  2. While lentils simmer, heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a large wide pot over medium-high heat. Add sliced onions and cook, stirring every few minutes, for 20–25 minutes until deep golden brown and jammy. Reserve a handful of the crispiest onions on a paper towel for topping.
  3. Add all spices to the remaining onions. Stir and cook 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Add par-cooked lentils, rinsed rice, and 1½ cups water or broth. Stir to combine. Bring to a boil, reduce to lowest setting, cover tightly, and cook 20 minutes. Do not lift the lid.
  5. Remove from heat. Keep covered and let steam 10 minutes. While it rests, toss diced cucumber and tomatoes with lemon juice, a drizzle of olive oil, and a pinch of salt.
  6. Fluff gently with a fork. Taste and adjust salt. Serve topped with crispy onions alongside yogurt and the cucumber-tomato salad.
🕐 Make-Ahead Options:
  • Night before: Caramelize the onions — keeps in the fridge up to 5 days. Day-of cooking is entirely hands-off.
  • Full dish: Make up to 3 days ahead. Reheat with a splash of water. Flavors deepen overnight.
  • Salad: Dice the veg the night before; dress just before serving.
😬 For baby (12 months+): Wonderful baby food — soft, slightly sticky texture easy to eat from a tray. Mild spices great for palate development. Skip the crispy onion topping. High in iron, protein, and fiber.
Storage & Freezer: Keeps 4–5 days in the fridge; gets better on day 2. Add a splash of water when reheating. Freezes well up to 3 months. Store crispy onions separately at room temp.
⚡ The caramelized onions are the key: Don’t shortcut them. 20–25 minutes of patient cooking is what makes this extraordinary. Put on a podcast and let them do their thing.

Meal 3: Smoky Black Bean & Cheese Enchiladas

Serves 4–5 | Active time: ~15 min | Total: ~40 min | Freezer-friendly
▼

Saucy, cheesy, deeply satisfying enchiladas with smoky cumin-spiced black beans. Mostly hands-off once they're in the oven, so you can set the table and get baby sorted while dinner finishes itself.

Ingredients

For the filling:

  • 2 cans (15 oz each) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ½ teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For assembly:

  • 8–10 flour tortillas (8-inch)
  • 1 can (28 oz) red enchilada sauce
  • 2 cups shredded Mexican cheese blend (or cheddar)

Optional toppings:

  • Sour cream
  • Avocado or guacamole
  • Fresh cilantro
  • Lime wedges

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F.
  2. Heat oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add onion and cook 4–5 minutes until softened. Add garlic and cook 1 minute more.
  3. Add cumin, smoked paprika, and chili powder. Stir 30 seconds until fragrant. Add black beans and stir to coat. Roughly mash half the beans with the back of a spoon — this makes a sturdier filling. Cook 2–3 more minutes. Season with salt and pepper and remove from heat.
  4. Pour about ½ cup of enchilada sauce into the bottom of a 9×13-inch baking dish and spread to coat.
  5. Lay a tortilla flat. Spoon 3–4 tablespoons of filling down the center, add a pinch of cheese (reserve most for the top). Roll up tightly and place seam-side down in the dish. Repeat with remaining tortillas.
  6. Pour remaining enchilada sauce evenly over the top. Sprinkle remaining cheese generously over everything.
  7. Cover tightly with foil and bake 20 minutes. Remove foil and bake 5 more minutes until cheese is bubbly and browning at the edges.
  8. Let rest 5 minutes before serving. Top with sour cream, avocado, and cilantro as desired.
🍼 For baby (12 months+): Cut a section of enchilada into small pieces — the tortilla softens beautifully in the sauce and is easy to eat. The beans are soft and high in iron. Sour cream and avocado are great additions. Skip any spicy toppings.
Storage & Freezer: Leftovers keep 4 days in the fridge and get better as the tortillas absorb the sauce. To freeze unbaked: assemble, cover tightly with foil, and freeze up to 3 months. Bake from frozen at 375°F for 45–50 minutes covered, then 10 minutes uncovered.
⚡ Speed tip: The bean filling takes 10 minutes. While it cools slightly, preheat the oven and set up your assembly line — sauce, beans, cheese, tortillas. Assembly goes fast once everything is laid out.

Chickpea Nuggets

Baby Lunch | Makes ~20–24 nuggets | Prep: 10 min | Bake: 20 min
▼

Crispy on the outside, soft on the inside, and loaded with protein and iron. A perfect plant-powered finger food for little ones and a great daycare lunch that reheats well. Adults love these too with a dipping sauce.

Ingredients

  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • ½ cup breadcrumbs (plain or panko)
  • 1 tablespoon tahini
  • 1 egg
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • ½ teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ¼ teaspoon cumin
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley or cilantro, finely chopped (optional)
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (for brushing)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Add the drained chickpeas to a mixing bowl. Mash with a fork or potato masher until mostly broken down but still a bit chunky — keep some texture. Don't over-mash; a few whole chickpeas left are totally fine.
  3. Add breadcrumbs, tahini, egg, garlic powder, smoked paprika, cumin, parsley (if using), and a pinch of salt. Stir everything together until it forms a thick, scoopable mixture. If too wet, add another tablespoon of breadcrumbs. If too dry, add a tiny splash of water.
  4. Using about 1 tablespoon of mixture per nugget, shape into small patties or ovals and place on the baking sheet. Flatten slightly to about ½ inch thick so they cook evenly. Brush or lightly drizzle the tops with olive oil.
  5. Bake 10 minutes, then carefully flip each nugget. Bake another 8–10 minutes until both sides are golden and firm to the touch.
  6. Let cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes — they firm up more as they cool. For daycare, pack 4–5 nuggets in a container. Great on their own or with a small side of hummus for dipping.
🍼 For baby (12 months+): These are sized and textured perfectly for this age. The slightly chunky texture is great for developing chewing skills. Serve at room temperature — no sauce needed, but a little yogurt or hummus on the side is great for dipping practice. Excellent iron and protein sources from the chickpeas and tahini.
Storage & Freezer: Keep 4 days in the fridge. Reheat in a toaster oven for a crispier result, or microwave for convenience. To freeze: cool completely, freeze in a single layer on parchment, then bag. Reheat from frozen in the microwave for 45–60 seconds or in a toaster oven. Keeps up to 3 months.
💡 Variations: Swap tahini for peanut butter or sunflower seed butter. Mix in ¼ cup finely grated carrot or zucchini (squeeze out moisture first). For a cheesy version, add 2 tbsp grated parmesan. Swap smoked paprika for mild curry powder for a different flavour profile.
