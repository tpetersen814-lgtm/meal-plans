Meal 1: Beyond Smash Burgers with Fries & Green Beans

Serves 4–5 | Prep: 5 min | Total: ~30 min | Oven fries + stovetop burgers
▼

Crispy smashed plant-based burgers with melted cheese, all the classic toppings, frozen fries in the oven, and quick garlicky green beans on the side. The key to a great smash burger is a very hot, dry pan and pressing the patty down hard — you get a lacey, caramelised crust that's better than anything you'd get from a grill.

Ingredients

For the burgers:

  • 4–5 Beyond Burger patties (or Impossible)
  • 4–5 burger buns, toasted
  • 4–5 slices American or cheddar cheese
  • 1 teaspoon vegetable oil (just enough to wipe the pan)
  • Salt and black pepper

Toppings (set out and let everyone build their own):

  • Lettuce
  • Tomato, sliced
  • Red onion, thinly sliced
  • Pickles
  • Ketchup, mustard, mayo
  • Sriracha mayo (mix equal parts mayo and sriracha) — optional

For the fries:

  • 1 bag (28–32 oz) frozen French fries

For the green beans:

  • 1 lb fresh or frozen green beans, trimmed
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Squeeze of lemon (optional)

Instructions

  1. Start the fries: preheat oven according to fries package directions (usually 425°F). Spread fries in a single layer on a baking sheet — don't overlap or they won't crisp. Put them in the oven. They'll cook while you make everything else.
  2. While fries cook, prep the burger toppings: slice tomato, red onion, and set out condiments. Toast buns in a dry skillet or toaster.
  3. Make the green beans: heat butter and olive oil in a wide skillet over medium-high heat. Add green beans and cook, tossing occasionally, for 5–6 minutes until blistered and just tender. Add garlic, toss, and cook 1 minute more. Season generously with salt and pepper. Finish with a squeeze of lemon if using. Set aside and keep warm.
  4. Cook the burgers: wipe the skillet with a tiny amount of vegetable oil on a paper towel and heat over the highest heat your stove allows until it just starts to smoke. Season each patty generously with salt and pepper. Working one or two at a time, place a patty in the pan and immediately press down hard with a spatula or burger press — really lean into it. Hold for 15–20 seconds.
  5. Cook for 2 minutes without moving. The edges will look lacy and deeply browned. Flip once, immediately place a cheese slice on top, and cook 1 more minute. The crust is the whole point — don't rush it.
  6. Build the burgers and serve immediately with fries and green beans.
🕐 Timing tip: Start fries first — they take the longest. While fries cook, do toppings, then green beans, then burgers last. Everything lands on the table at the same time with almost no stress. Total active cooking time is about 15 minutes.
🍼 For baby (12 months+): The green beans are perfect for baby — soft enough to eat but with some texture for chewing practice. Offer a few plain beans on the tray. A small piece of burger patty cut into tiny pieces is fine — Beyond/Impossible is high in protein and iron. Skip the bun or offer a small piece of soft bread on the side. Fries are fine in small amounts; cut into shorter pieces and skip any seasoned/spicy varieties. Skip the sriracha mayo and pickles for baby.
Storage: Cooked burger patties keep 3 days in the fridge — reheat in a hot skillet for best results. Green beans keep 3–4 days. Fries don't reheat well; make only what you'll eat. Beyond/Impossible patties can be refrozen if they were thawed in the fridge (not at room temperature).
🔥 The smash technique: The pan needs to be genuinely very hot — almost smoking. A cast iron skillet is ideal but any heavy pan works. The hard press creates maximum surface contact and builds that deep caramelised crust. Don't be shy with it.

Meal 2: Sesame Garlic Noodles with Plant-Based Chicken

Serves 4–5 | Prep: 8 min | Total: ~25 min | One pan + one pot
▼

Silky noodles tossed in a rich, garlicky sesame sauce with crispy plant-based chicken strips and crunchy vegetables. The sauce comes together in a bowl while the noodles cook and the chicken crisps up in the same pan. Punchy, satisfying, and genuinely quick.

Ingredients

For the noodles and chicken:

  • 12 oz lo mein noodles, spaghetti, or linguine
  • 1 package (9–10 oz) plant-based chicken strips (Gardein, MorningStar, or similar)
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 2 cups shredded red cabbage
  • 2 medium carrots, julienned or shredded
  • 4 green onions, sliced (white and green parts separated)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds, for garnish
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish (optional)
  • Lime wedges, to serve

For the sesame garlic sauce:

  • ⅓ cup soy sauce
  • 3 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar or maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon peanut butter (smooth)
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced or grated
  • 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, minced or grated
  • 2 tablespoons warm water (to loosen)
  • 1–2 teaspoons sriracha (optional)

Instructions

  1. Make the sauce: whisk together soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, brown sugar, peanut butter, garlic, ginger, warm water, and sriracha if using until smooth. Set aside. This is the only real prep — just mince garlic and ginger.
  2. Cook noodles according to package directions until just al dente. Before draining, reserve ¼ cup of the pasta water. Drain and rinse noodles briefly with cold water to stop cooking. Set aside.
  3. While noodles cook, heat oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add plant-based chicken strips and cook according to package directions — usually 4–5 minutes, turning once, until golden and heated through. Remove and set aside.
  4. In the same pan over medium heat, add the white parts of the green onions and cook 1 minute. Add the shredded cabbage and carrots. Stir-fry for 2–3 minutes until just slightly softened but still with some crunch.
  5. Add the cooked noodles to the pan. Pour the sesame garlic sauce over everything. Toss well to coat, adding a splash of the reserved pasta water if the sauce is too thick. Cook 1–2 minutes until everything is hot and glossy.
  6. Return the crispy plant-based chicken to the pan and toss to combine. Serve in bowls topped with green onion tops, sesame seeds, and cilantro. Lime wedges on the side.
🕐 Make-Ahead: The sauce can be made up to 5 days ahead and kept in a jar in the fridge — it actually gets better as the garlic mellows. Shred the cabbage and carrots the night before and store in a container. Day-of prep is then just cooking noodles and chicken, which is maybe 8 minutes of active work.
🍼 For baby (12 months+): Set aside some plain noodles and cooked cabbage/carrots before tossing with the soy-heavy sauce (high sodium). Baby can have the plain noodles with a tiny drizzle of sesame oil for flavour. The plant-based chicken is fine in small pieces — check the brand's sodium content and offer a small amount. Skip the sriracha entirely for baby.
Storage: Keeps 4 days in the fridge. Noodles absorb sauce as they sit — add a splash of water or extra soy sauce when reheating and toss in a hot skillet or microwave. The sauce alone keeps up to 1 week refrigerated and can be used on grain bowls, salads, or as a dipping sauce.
💡 Noodle tip: Lo mein noodles from the Asian aisle give the best result — they're thick and silky and hold the sauce beautifully. Spaghetti or linguine work perfectly well as substitutes if that's what you have. Don't overcook — pull them just before they're done as they'll finish in the pan.

👶 Baby Breakfast

Banana Oat Pancakes

Baby Breakfast | Makes ~16–20 small pancakes | Prep: 5 min | Cook: 15 min
▼

Simple 4-ingredient pancakes perfect for little ones. Naturally sweet, soft, and easy for small hands to grab. Freezes beautifully — make a big batch on the weekend for quick daycare breakfasts all week.

Ingredients

  • 2 ripe bananas
  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 2 eggs
  • ½ teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon butter or oil (for the pan)

Instructions

  1. Add bananas (broken into chunks), oats, eggs, and cinnamon to a blender or food processor. Blend until smooth, about 20–30 seconds. The batter will be thinner than traditional pancake batter — that's normal. Let it rest 5 minutes so the oats absorb liquid and the batter thickens slightly.
  2. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium-low heat. Add butter or oil and spread around. Medium-low is important — these brown faster than regular pancakes because of the banana sugars.
  3. Pour about 1 tablespoon of batter per pancake (silver-dollar size). Cook until bubbles form on the surface and edges look set, about 2 minutes.
  4. Gently flip each pancake and cook 1–2 more minutes until golden on the bottom. Use a thin spatula — these are delicate. Transfer to a plate or cooling rack.
  5. Cool completely before storing. For daycare: pack 3–4 in a small container. Great cold or microwaved for 20–30 seconds.
🍼 For baby (12 months+): These were designed for baby! Serve at room temperature — small enough to grab, soft enough to gum. Add 1 tbsp peanut butter to the blender for extra protein and healthy fat. Also great with a small smear of yogurt on top.
Freezer Instructions: Cool completely, lay in a single layer on a parchment-lined sheet and freeze 1–2 hours, then transfer to a freezer bag. Keeps up to 3 months. Microwave straight from frozen for 30–45 seconds, or pull out the night before.
💡 Variations: Toss a small handful of spinach into the blender — it disappears in the batter and adds iron. For adults: top with yogurt and fresh berries.
