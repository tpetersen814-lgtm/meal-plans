Meal 1: Lemon Orzo with White Beans & Spinach

Serves 4–5 | Prep: 5 min | Total: ~25 min | One pot
▼

A bright, lemony one-pot pasta that comes together incredibly fast. Orzo cooks right in the broth, absorbing all the flavour, and the white beans and spinach make it hearty enough to be a complete meal. Almost no chopping required — just garlic and you're basically done.

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups (about 10 oz) orzo pasta
  • 1 can (15 oz) white beans (cannellini), drained and rinsed
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup water
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 3 cups baby spinach
  • Zest and juice of 1 large lemon
  • ½ cup grated parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • ½ teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Extra parmesan and lemon wedges to serve

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot or deep skillet over medium heat. Add garlic and cook 1–2 minutes, stirring, until fragrant and just starting to turn golden. Don't let it burn.
  2. Add orzo and stir to coat in the oil. Toast for 1 minute — this adds a little nuttiness.
  3. Pour in vegetable broth and water. Add oregano and a generous pinch of salt. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to a lively simmer. Cook uncovered for 8–10 minutes, stirring frequently (every minute or so), until orzo is just tender and has absorbed most of the liquid. It should look saucy, not dry.
  4. Stir in white beans and spinach. Cook 1–2 minutes until spinach is wilted and beans are warmed through.
  5. Remove from heat. Stir in butter, lemon zest, lemon juice, and parmesan. Taste and adjust salt, pepper, and lemon — it should be bright and savoury. If it thickens too much as it sits, add a splash of water or broth.
  6. Serve immediately topped with extra parmesan, a crack of black pepper, and red pepper flakes if using.
🍼 For baby (12 months+): Orzo is an ideal pasta shape for babies — small, soft, and easy to pick up. Scoop out a portion before adding the lemon and parmesan if you'd like a blander version, or serve as-is (the lemon is mild once cooked in). The white beans are soft and high in iron — offer them whole or lightly squished. Great self-feeding meal.
Storage: Keeps 3–4 days in the fridge. Orzo absorbs liquid as it sits — add a good splash of broth or water when reheating and stir over medium heat or microwave until loosened. Doesn't freeze well (pasta texture suffers), so best eaten fresh or within a few days.
⚡ Speed tip: Seriously minimal prep — just mince the garlic and zest the lemon. Everything else is canned or dried. This is a legitimate 25-minute dinner with under 5 minutes of knife work.

Meal 2: Baked Feta with Tomatoes & Chickpeas

Serves 4–5 | Prep: 5 min | Total: ~35 min | Hands-off oven dinner
▼

Inspired by the viral baked feta pasta trend but heartier — a block of feta roasts in a dish surrounded by burst cherry tomatoes, chickpeas, and olive oil until everything is jammy and rich. Scoop it all up with crusty bread or toss with pasta. Almost zero prep: open cans, add feta, put in oven.

Ingredients

For the bake:

  • 1 block (7–8 oz) feta cheese
  • 2 pints (about 4 cups) cherry tomatoes
  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 5 cloves garlic, left whole or lightly smashed
  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • ½ teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ½ teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Black pepper to taste

To serve (pick one or both):

  • Crusty bread for scooping
  • 10 oz pasta (rigatoni or penne), cooked and reserved
  • Fresh basil leaves

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F.
  2. In a large baking dish (9×13 or similar), combine cherry tomatoes, chickpeas, and garlic cloves. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with oregano, smoked paprika, red pepper flakes (if using), and black pepper. Toss to coat.
  3. Push the tomato and chickpea mixture to the sides to create a space in the centre. Place the block of feta in the middle. Drizzle a little extra olive oil over the feta.
  4. Bake for 25–30 minutes until the tomatoes have burst and are jammy, the chickpeas are slightly crispy at the edges, and the feta is soft and golden on top.
  5. Remove from oven. Use a spoon to mash the feta slightly and stir everything together into a chunky, saucy mixture. The tomato juices and olive oil will combine with the feta into a rich sauce.
  6. Serve immediately: either with crusty bread for scooping directly from the dish, or toss with cooked pasta and a splash of pasta water to loosen. Top with fresh basil.
🕐 Make-Ahead: The dish takes 5 minutes to assemble. You can do that in the morning and refrigerate the whole unbaked dish, then put it straight in the oven when you get home. By the time you've got baby sorted and set the table it'll be done.
🍼 For baby (12 months+): Scoop out some chickpeas and burst tomatoes before mixing in the salty feta — they're soft and perfect. Or offer a small amount of the full mixture; feta is fine for this age in moderate amounts. If serving with pasta, small pasta shapes are great for self-feeding. Avoid the red pepper flakes for baby.
Storage: Leftovers keep 3–4 days in the fridge and are excellent reheated — the flavours deepen overnight. Reheat in a small saucepan over medium-low heat with a splash of water, or microwave. If you mixed it with pasta, add a little water or broth to loosen.
⚡ Truly minimal prep: This is genuinely a 5-minute assembly job. Open cans, rinse chickpeas, tip in tomatoes, place feta, drizzle oil, season, bake. The oven does everything. Great night to give baby a bath while dinner cooks itself.

Meal 3: Paneer Tikka Masala

Serves 4–5 | Prep: 10 min | Total: ~40 min | Serve with rice or naan
▼

Rich, fragrant tomato-cream sauce with golden pan-fried paneer. This is a genuine crowd-pleaser and one of the most satisfying vegetarian dinners you can make. The paneer takes 5 minutes to cube, and the sauce is mostly built from pantry spices and canned tomatoes — the simmering time does the heavy lifting.

Ingredients

For the paneer:

  • 1 block (14–16 oz) paneer, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric
  • ½ teaspoon smoked paprika

For the sauce:

  • 1 medium onion, finely diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, minced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • ½ cup heavy cream (or full-fat coconut milk for a dairy-free version)
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1½ teaspoons garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon coriander
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric
  • ½ teaspoon sugar
  • Salt to taste

To serve:

  • 2 cups dry basmati rice, cooked
  • Naan bread (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro

Instructions

  1. Start the rice cooking according to package directions — it'll be ready when the sauce is done.
  2. Toss paneer cubes with turmeric and smoked paprika. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add paneer in a single layer and cook 2–3 minutes per side, turning occasionally, until golden on most sides. Remove and set aside — don't worry about cooking it through, it'll finish in the sauce.
  3. In the same pan, add remaining oil over medium heat. Add onion and cook 6–7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until soft and lightly golden.
  4. Add garlic and ginger. Cook 1–2 minutes until fragrant. Add tomato paste and stir, cooking 1 minute more.
  5. Add garam masala, cumin, coriander, smoked paprika, and turmeric. Stir and cook 1 minute — the spices will become very fragrant.
  6. Add diced tomatoes and sugar. Stir everything together, scraping any bits from the bottom of the pan. Bring to a simmer and cook 8–10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce has thickened and deepened in colour.
  7. Stir in heavy cream. Add the golden paneer back to the pan. Simmer gently for 5 minutes until the paneer is heated through and the sauce is rich and glossy. Taste and season with salt.
  8. Serve over basmati rice with naan if using. Top with fresh cilantro.
🕐 Make-Ahead: The sauce (without paneer) can be made up to 3 days ahead and refrigerated, or frozen for up to 3 months. Reheat the sauce, then add freshly pan-fried paneer at the end. This is actually the best approach — the sauce develops even more flavour overnight.
🍼 For baby (12 months+): Paneer is an excellent baby food — high in protein and calcium, and soft enough to eat easily. Set aside a piece or two of paneer before adding to the spiced sauce, or offer a small portion of the full dish (the spices are flavourful but not hot). Serve with soft rice. The sauce is rich and great for exposing baby to complex spice flavours early.
Storage & Freezer: Leftovers keep 4 days in the fridge and taste even better the next day. Freeze the sauce (without paneer) for up to 3 months — paneer can turn rubbery after freezing, so add fresh paneer when reheating from frozen. Reheat gently over low heat with a splash of water.
⚡ The main prep: Dicing one onion and mincing garlic and ginger is the bulk of the work — about 8–10 minutes. After that the sauce simmers mostly hands-off while you get baby ready for dinner.

👶 Baby Breakfast

Banana Oat Pancakes

Baby Breakfast | Makes ~16–20 small pancakes | Prep: 5 min | Cook: 15 min
▼

Simple 4-ingredient pancakes perfect for little ones. Naturally sweet, soft, and easy for small hands to grab. Freezes beautifully — make a big batch on the weekend for quick daycare breakfasts all week.

Ingredients

  • 2 ripe bananas
  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 2 eggs
  • ½ teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon butter or oil (for the pan)

Instructions

  1. Add bananas (broken into chunks), oats, eggs, and cinnamon to a blender or food processor. Blend until smooth, about 20–30 seconds. The batter will be thinner than traditional pancake batter — that's normal. Let it rest 5 minutes so the oats absorb liquid and the batter thickens slightly.
  2. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium-low heat. Add butter or oil and spread around. Medium-low is important — these brown faster than regular pancakes because of the banana sugars.
  3. Pour about 1 tablespoon of batter per pancake (silver-dollar size). Cook until bubbles form on the surface and edges look set, about 2 minutes.
  4. Gently flip each pancake and cook 1–2 more minutes until golden on the bottom. Use a thin spatula — these are delicate. Transfer to a plate or cooling rack.
  5. Let cool completely before storing. For daycare: pack 3–4 in a small container. Great cold or microwaved for 20–30 seconds.
🍼 For baby (12 months+): These were designed for baby! Serve at room temperature. Great for self-feeding — small enough to grab, soft enough to gum. Add 1 tbsp of peanut butter to the blender for extra protein and healthy fat. Also excellent with a small smear of yogurt on top.
Freezer Instructions: Cool completely, lay in a single layer on a parchment-lined baking sheet and freeze 1–2 hours, then transfer to a freezer bag. Keeps up to 3 months. Microwave straight from frozen for 30–45 seconds, or pull out the night before.
💡 Variations: Toss a small handful of spinach into the blender — it disappears in the batter and adds iron. For adults: top with yogurt and fresh berries.
