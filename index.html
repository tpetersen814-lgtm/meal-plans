🍽️ Weeknight Dinners

Meal 1: Tofu Egg Fried Rice

Serves 4–5 | Prep: 10 min | Total: ~25 min | Best with day-old rice
▼

The ultimate use-what-you-have weeknight dinner. Crispy tofu, scrambled egg, and vegetables tossed through savoury fried rice. The key to great fried rice is using cold, day-old rice — freshly cooked rice is too wet and steams instead of frying. Cook the rice the night before and this comes together in 20 minutes flat.

Ingredients

For the rice and tofu:

  • 4 cups cooked and cooled white rice (ideally day-old — about 1½ cups dry)
  • 1 block (14 oz) extra-firm tofu, pressed and cut into small cubes
  • 3 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 2 cups frozen peas and carrots (or any frozen veg mix)
  • 1 cup frozen corn kernels
  • 4 green onions, sliced (white and green parts separated)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, minced
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided

For the sauce:

  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sriracha or chili garlic sauce (optional)

To finish:

  • Extra soy sauce to taste
  • Sesame seeds (optional)
  • Extra green onion tops

Instructions

  1. Mix the sauce: stir together soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, and sriracha if using. Set aside. Press tofu between paper towels for a few minutes to remove moisture, then cut into small ½-inch cubes.
  2. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a large wok or wide skillet over high heat until very hot. Add tofu in a single layer and cook without stirring for 3–4 minutes until deeply golden on the bottom. Flip and cook another 2 minutes. Remove and set aside.
  3. Add another tablespoon of oil to the pan. Add the frozen veg and corn — they'll sizzle and steam. Stir-fry for 2–3 minutes until thawed and any water has evaporated. Add the white parts of the green onions, garlic, and ginger. Stir-fry 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Push everything to the sides. Add the remaining oil to the centre of the pan. Pour in the beaten eggs and scramble, stirring quickly. Once just set but still a little soft, break them up and fold into the vegetables.
  5. Add the cold rice. Press it down and let it sit undisturbed for 1 minute to get a little crispy on the bottom. Then toss and stir-fry for 2–3 minutes, breaking up any clumps, until the rice is hot and slightly toasted.
  6. Return the crispy tofu to the pan. Pour the sauce over everything and toss well to coat. Taste and add more soy sauce if needed. Remove from heat and finish with sesame seeds and green onion tops. Serve immediately.
🕐 Make-Ahead: Cook the rice the night before — this is actually essential for good fried rice. Spread it on a tray to cool, then refrigerate uncovered. Cold, dry rice fries beautifully; fresh warm rice turns mushy. You can also press and cube the tofu the night before.
🍼 For baby (12 months+): Set aside a small portion of rice, veg, and tofu before adding the soy sauce (which is high in sodium). Baby can have plain rice with the scrambled egg and soft veg mixed in — excellent self-feeding meal. Tofu cubes are a great texture for this age. If you do offer a small taste of the sauced version, it's fine occasionally — just go easy on the portions.
Storage: Keeps 4 days in the fridge. Reheat in a hot skillet with a tiny splash of water or oil for best results — the microwave works but the rice can go a bit soft. Freezes reasonably well for up to 1 month, though the texture is best fresh.
⚡ The real secret: Day-old rice is non-negotiable for good fried rice. If you forget, spread freshly cooked rice on a baking sheet and put it in the freezer for 20–30 minutes to dry it out fast. Works surprisingly well.

Meal 2: Mushroom Stroganoff

Serves 4–5 | Prep: 10 min | Total: ~35 min | Serve over egg noodles or rice
▼

Rich, creamy, deeply savoury mushroom sauce over wide egg noodles. This is proper comfort food — the mushrooms take about 10 minutes of prep (slicing) and then the sauce comes together quickly. Using a mix of mushrooms adds much more depth than just button mushrooms, but it works fine with whatever you have.

Ingredients

For the stroganoff:

  • 1½ lbs mixed mushrooms (cremini, shiitake, and/or portobello), sliced
  • 1 medium onion, finely diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • ¾ cup sour cream
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce (check label — most are vegetarian, some contain anchovies)
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ½ teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, to serve

To serve:

  • 12 oz wide egg noodles (or pappardelle, or serve over rice)

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil for the noodles. Slice the mushrooms while it heats.
  2. Heat butter and olive oil in a large wide skillet over medium-high heat. Add onion and cook 4–5 minutes until softened. Add garlic and cook 1 minute more.
  3. Add the sliced mushrooms in one go — they'll look like too many but will shrink dramatically. Cook without stirring for 3–4 minutes to let them brown on the bottom, then stir and cook another 4–5 minutes until deeply golden and all the liquid they release has evaporated. This is the most important step — don't rush it. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Sprinkle flour over the mushrooms and stir to coat. Cook 1 minute. Add smoked paprika and dried thyme and stir again.
  5. Pour in the vegetable broth, scraping any bits from the bottom of the pan. Add Dijon mustard and Worcestershire sauce. Stir everything together and bring to a gentle simmer. Cook 3–4 minutes until the sauce has thickened slightly.
  6. Cook the noodles according to package directions while the sauce simmers.
  7. Remove the pan from heat. Stir in the sour cream — don't add it over high heat or it may curdle. Taste and adjust seasoning. Add a splash of broth if the sauce is too thick.
  8. Serve immediately over drained noodles, topped generously with fresh parsley.
🕐 Make-Ahead: The stroganoff sauce keeps well in the fridge for 3 days — make it ahead and reheat gently over low heat, adding a splash of broth to loosen. Cook the noodles fresh when serving. The sauce also makes a great pasta topping or can be spooned over baked potatoes for quick lunches.
🍼 For baby (12 months+): Mushroom stroganoff is actually a great baby food — the mushrooms are soft and the sauce is mild and creamy. Set aside a small portion of mushrooms and sauce before adding the Worcestershire sauce (for lower sodium). Serve over small pasta or rice cut to appropriate size. The sour cream sauce is rich and good for baby's calorie needs. Chop mushrooms into small pieces for this age.
Storage: Sauce keeps 3–4 days in the fridge. Store separately from the noodles — noodles absorb the sauce and go mushy. Reheat sauce gently over low heat with a splash of broth, then cook fresh noodles. The sauce freezes well for up to 2 months; add the sour cream fresh after reheating rather than freezing it in.
⚡ Mushroom tip: The prep time here is mostly slicing mushrooms — about 8–10 minutes for 1½ lbs. Don't wipe them with a wet cloth before slicing; a quick brush or dry paper towel is fine. Wet mushrooms steam instead of browning.

👶 Baby Breakfast

Banana Oat Pancakes

Baby Breakfast | Makes ~16–20 small pancakes | Prep: 5 min | Cook: 15 min
▼

Simple 4-ingredient pancakes perfect for little ones. Naturally sweet, soft, and easy for small hands to grab. Freezes beautifully — make a big batch on the weekend for quick daycare breakfasts all week.

Ingredients

  • 2 ripe bananas
  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 2 eggs
  • ½ teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon butter or oil (for the pan)

Instructions

  1. Add bananas (broken into chunks), oats, eggs, and cinnamon to a blender or food processor. Blend until smooth, about 20–30 seconds. The batter will be thinner than traditional pancake batter — that's normal. Let it rest 5 minutes so the oats absorb liquid and the batter thickens slightly.
  2. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium-low heat. Add butter or oil and spread around. Medium-low is important — these brown faster than regular pancakes because of the banana sugars.
  3. Pour about 1 tablespoon of batter per pancake (silver-dollar size). Cook until bubbles form on the surface and edges look set, about 2 minutes.
  4. Gently flip each pancake and cook 1–2 more minutes until golden on the bottom. Use a thin spatula — these are delicate. Transfer to a plate or cooling rack.
  5. Cool completely before storing. For daycare: pack 3–4 in a small container. Great cold or microwaved for 20–30 seconds.
🍼 For baby (12 months+): These were designed for baby! Serve at room temperature — small enough to grab, soft enough to gum. Add 1 tbsp peanut butter to the blender for extra protein and healthy fat. Also great with a small smear of yogurt on top.
Freezer Instructions: Cool completely, lay in a single layer on a parchment-lined sheet and freeze 1–2 hours, then transfer to a freezer bag. Keeps up to 3 months. Microwave straight from frozen for 30–45 seconds, or pull out the night before.
💡 Variations: Toss a small handful of spinach into the blender — it disappears in the batter and adds iron. For adults: top with yogurt and fresh berries.
