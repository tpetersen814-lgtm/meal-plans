Meal 1: Saag Paneer

Serves 4–5 | Prep: 10 min | Total: ~35 min | Serve with rice or naan
▼

One of the great Indian classics — golden pan-fried paneer nestled in a deeply spiced, silky creamed spinach. The spinach gets blended into a smooth sauce that carries warmth from ginger, garlic, and garam masala. A great way to use a large quantity of spinach at once. Serve with basmati rice and naan.

Ingredients

For the paneer:

  • 1 block (14–16 oz) paneer, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric

For the saag:

  • 1½ lbs (about 10–12 cups) fresh spinach — or 2 packages frozen spinach, thawed and squeezed dry
  • 1 medium onion, finely diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, minced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 3 tablespoons plain whole milk yogurt or heavy cream
  • 1½ teaspoons garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon coriander
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric
  • ½ teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ¼ teaspoon cayenne (optional — omit for a milder version)
  • 1 teaspoon salt, or to taste
  • 1 tablespoon butter (for finishing)

To serve:

  • 2 cups dry basmati rice, cooked
  • Naan bread (optional)
  • Plain yogurt
  • Lemon wedges

Instructions

  1. Start the rice. Cook basmati according to package directions — it'll be ready when the saag is done.
  2. Pan-fry the paneer: toss paneer cubes with turmeric. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add paneer in a single layer and cook 2–3 minutes per side until golden. Remove and set aside.
  3. Wilt the spinach: in the same pan over medium heat, add spinach in large handfuls, stirring between additions, until fully wilted — about 3–4 minutes. (If using frozen, skip this step.) Transfer to a blender and blend until smooth. Set aside.
  4. Make the masala base: heat 2 tablespoons oil in the same pan over medium heat. Add onion and cook 6–7 minutes until soft and golden. Add garlic and ginger, cook 1–2 minutes until fragrant.
  5. Add garam masala, cumin, coriander, turmeric, smoked paprika, and cayenne if using. Stir and cook 1 minute until the spices are fragrant. Add diced tomatoes (with liquid) and cook 4–5 minutes, stirring, until the sauce thickens and the tomatoes break down.
  6. Add the blended spinach to the pan and stir to combine. Simmer 5 minutes. Stir in yogurt or cream. Season with salt. If the sauce is very thick, add a splash of water to loosen.
  7. Add the golden paneer back to the pan. Stir gently and simmer 3–4 minutes until the paneer is heated through and has absorbed some of the sauce. Finish with a tablespoon of butter stirred through — this adds a beautiful richness.
  8. Serve over basmati rice with naan, a dollop of plain yogurt, and lemon wedges.
🕐 Make-Ahead: The spinach sauce (without paneer) can be made 3 days ahead and refrigerated, or frozen for up to 3 months. Pan-fry fresh paneer when serving and add to the reheated sauce — the paneer is best cooked fresh as it can turn rubbery after freezing. This is actually the ideal approach as the sauce deepens beautifully overnight.
🍼 For baby (14 months+): Saag paneer is a wonderful baby meal — the spinach sauce is smooth and nutritious, and paneer is soft, high in protein and calcium. Cut paneer into small pieces appropriate for baby's chewing ability. Skip the cayenne in baby's portion (or make the whole dish without it and add chili flakes to adult plates). The garam masala and warming spices are mild and excellent for palate development. Serve with soft rice.
Storage & Freezer: Keeps 4 days in the fridge. Reheat gently over low heat with a splash of water. Freeze the sauce without paneer — up to 3 months. Add freshly cooked paneer when serving from frozen.
💡 Spinach tip: Fresh spinach wilts down dramatically — 10–12 cups becomes about 1 cup of puree. This is normal! Frozen spinach is a great shortcut here: 2 packages (thawed and squeezed completely dry) works perfectly and saves 5 minutes of wilting time.

Meal 2: Beyond Meat Chili

Serves 4–5 | Prep: 10 min | Total: ~40 min | Excellent freezer meal
▼

A deeply spiced, one-pot chili with plant-based ground meat, two kinds of beans, and smoky chipotle heat. One of the best freezer meals in the rotation — make a big batch and you've got easy weeknight dinners and lunches sorted for weeks. Gets noticeably better the next day.

Ingredients

For the chili:

  • 1 lb Beyond Meat ground (or Impossible ground)
  • 2 cans (15 oz each) kidney or black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 medium onion, finely diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red or green bell pepper, diced
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • ½ cup vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 chipotle pepper in adobo sauce, minced (or ½ tsp chipotle powder)
  • 2 teaspoons chili powder
  • 1½ teaspoons cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ½ teaspoon dried oregano
  • ½ teaspoon sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

To serve:

  • Sour cream
  • Shredded cheddar cheese
  • Sliced green onions
  • Avocado or guacamole
  • Hot sauce
  • Warm rice or cornbread

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the Beyond Meat and cook, breaking it up with a spoon, for 4–5 minutes until browned and starting to crisp at the edges. Beyond browns better if you let it sit a moment before stirring. Remove and set aside.
  2. Reduce heat to medium. Add onion and bell pepper to the same pot. Cook 5–6 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened. Add garlic and cook 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Add tomato paste, chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, oregano, and chipotle. Stir and cook 1–2 minutes until the paste darkens slightly and the spices become very fragrant.
  4. Add crushed tomatoes, diced tomatoes (with liquid), vegetable broth, sugar, and the browned Beyond Meat. Stir well, scraping any bits from the bottom of the pot. Bring to a boil.
  5. Stir in the beans. Reduce heat to low, cover partially, and simmer for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally. The chili should be thick and deeply coloured. Taste and season generously with salt and pepper.
  6. Ladle into bowls and serve with sour cream, shredded cheese, green onions, avocado, and hot sauce. Great over rice or with cornbread on the side.
🕐 Make-Ahead: This chili is genuinely better the next day as the spices deepen. Make the full batch ahead and refrigerate — reheat in 10 minutes. Also ideal as a Sunday batch cook: make a double batch, eat some now, freeze the rest in individual portions for instant weeknight dinners.
🍼 For baby (14 months+): Before adding the chipotle, scoop out some beans, cooked onion, and bell pepper for baby — this is a great mild base with lots of iron and protein. The Beyond Meat is fine for baby in small amounts; chop any larger pieces. Mash the beans lightly if needed. Serve with soft rice. Skip the hot sauce and jalapeños.
Storage & Freezer: Keeps 5 days in the fridge. Freezes perfectly up to 3 months — freeze in individual portions in zip-lock bags laid flat. Reheat from frozen in a pot over low heat with a splash of water, or microwave. One of the best meals in the freezer rotation.
⚡ Beyond Meat tip: Beyond browns better than regular ground meat if you give it space and don't stir too early. Let it sit in the hot pan for a minute before breaking it up — you'll get better colour and more flavour.

👶 Baby Lunch (batch cook)

Sweet Potato & Lentil Patties

Baby Lunch | Makes ~20 patties | Prep: 15 min | Cook: 25 min | Freeze well
▼

Soft inside, lightly crispy outside, and packed with iron, protein, and natural sweetness. The red lentils cook down into the sweet potato to create a tender, flavourful patty that holds together well and is easy for a 14-month-old to pick up and eat independently. Make a big batch on the weekend and freeze — they reheat beautifully.

Ingredients

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes (about 1½ lbs), peeled and cubed
  • ¾ cup red lentils, rinsed
  • 1½ cups water or vegetable broth (for cooking lentils)
  • ½ cup breadcrumbs (plain), plus extra if needed
  • 1 egg
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • ½ teaspoon coriander
  • ½ teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ¼ teaspoon turmeric
  • Pinch of salt
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or vegetable oil (for cooking)

Optional mix-ins:

  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped fresh parsley or cilantro
  • ¼ cup finely grated carrot or zucchini (squeeze out moisture first)
  • 2 tablespoons grated parmesan

Instructions

  1. Cook the lentils: combine rinsed red lentils with water or broth in a small saucepan. Bring to a boil, reduce heat, and simmer uncovered for 12–15 minutes until completely soft and most liquid is absorbed. They should be very soft — almost mushy. Drain any excess liquid and set aside to cool slightly.
  2. Cook the sweet potato: while lentils cook, steam or microwave the sweet potato cubes until completely soft — about 8–10 minutes. (Microwave with a splash of water covered in cling wrap works well.) Drain well and mash thoroughly with a fork. Let cool slightly.
  3. Combine: in a large bowl, mix together the mashed sweet potato, cooked lentils, garlic, cumin, coriander, smoked paprika, turmeric, and a pinch of salt. Add the egg and breadcrumbs and mix until the mixture holds together when pressed. If it feels too wet to shape, add another tablespoon or two of breadcrumbs. If too dry, add a tiny splash of water. Fold in any optional mix-ins.
  4. Shape: using slightly damp hands, scoop about 2 tablespoons of mixture per patty. Press into a small disc about ½ inch thick — roughly the size of a large cookie. You should get around 20 patties.
  5. Cook: heat oil in a large non-stick skillet over medium heat. Cook patties in batches — 3–4 minutes per side until golden and slightly crispy on the outside. Don't move them around — let them form a crust before flipping. They'll firm up more as they cool.
  6. Cool completely before packing or freezing. For daycare: pack 3–4 patties per container. Great at room temperature or reheated in the microwave for 30–40 seconds.
🕐 Batch Cook Strategy: This is a Sunday batch-cook recipe. The lentils and sweet potato can be cooked and mashed the night before — just refrigerate the mixture and shape and cook on Sunday morning. The whole process from scratch takes about 40 minutes including cooling time. Make the full batch every 2–3 weeks and you're stocked.
🍼 For baby (14 months+): These are specifically designed for this age! At 14 months with good teeth, the slightly crispy exterior is great for chewing practice. The inside stays soft. The spices — cumin, coriander, turmeric — are all mild and excellent for building an adventurous palate. Great served with a small dipping portion of plain yogurt. High in iron from the lentils, vitamin A from sweet potato, and protein from the egg and lentils.
Freezer Instructions: Cool completely. Lay in a single layer on a parchment-lined baking sheet and freeze 1–2 hours. Transfer to a freezer bag — keeps up to 3 months. Reheat from frozen: microwave 45–60 seconds, or toaster oven at 350°F for 8–10 minutes for a crispier result. Pull out the night before to thaw in the fridge if preferred.
💡 Texture tip: The mixture gets easier to shape once it has cooled — warm sweet potato is stickier. If you're having trouble shaping, give the bowl 10 minutes in the fridge first. Wet hands also help a lot.
