Meal 1: Plant-Based Sausage & White Bean Stew

Serves 4–5 | Prep: 5 min | Total: ~25 min | One pot
▼

A hearty Italian-style stew with sliced plant-based sausage, creamy cannellini beans, canned tomatoes, and fragrant rosemary — all pulled together in one pot in under 30 minutes. Minimal chopping, maximum flavour. Serve with crusty bread to mop up the rich broth.

Ingredients

For the stew:

  • 1 package (12–14 oz) plant-based sausages (Italian-style — Field Roast, Beyond, or Tofurky work well), sliced into rounds
  • 2 cans (15 oz each) cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups baby spinach or kale, roughly chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 sprig fresh rosemary (or ½ tsp dried)
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • ½ teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ½ teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Parmesan rind (optional — adds incredible depth if you have one)

To serve:

  • Crusty bread or sourdough
  • Grated parmesan
  • Extra olive oil for drizzling

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the sliced sausage in a single layer and cook for 3–4 minutes, turning occasionally, until browned on most sides. Remove and set aside. (Getting good colour on the sausage is worth the extra minute — it adds a lot of flavour.)
  2. Reduce heat to medium. Add onion to the same pot and cook 3–4 minutes until softened. Add garlic and cook 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Add smoked paprika, oregano, and red pepper flakes if using. Stir 30 seconds. Add diced tomatoes (with liquid), vegetable broth, rosemary sprig, and parmesan rind if using. Stir to combine and bring to a simmer.
  4. Add cannellini beans. Stir everything together and simmer for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the broth has thickened slightly and the beans are warmed through. Use the back of a spoon to gently mash a few beans against the side of the pot — this thickens the stew beautifully.
  5. Remove the rosemary sprig and parmesan rind. Return the browned sausage to the pot. Stir in the spinach or kale and cook 1–2 minutes until just wilted. Taste and season generously with salt and pepper.
  6. Ladle into bowls, drizzle with olive oil, and top with grated parmesan. Serve with plenty of crusty bread.
🕐 Make-Ahead: This stew gets better as it sits — the beans absorb the broth and everything melds together. Make it up to 3 days ahead and reheat gently. Add a splash of broth when reheating as it thickens in the fridge. Add the spinach fresh when reheating so it doesn't go mushy.
🍼 For baby (14 months+): The cannellini beans are soft, mild, and excellent for baby — high in fibre and protein. Scoop out some beans and broth before adding the sausage (which can be high in sodium). Mash the beans slightly for a good texture. The spinach wilted into the broth is also great. Skip the red pepper flakes in baby's portion and go easy on the parmesan (salty).
Storage & Freezer: Keeps 4–5 days in the fridge. Freezes well for up to 3 months — freeze without the spinach and add fresh when reheating. The beans will absorb more liquid as it sits; add a splash of broth when reheating.
💡 Parmesan rind tip: If you keep parmesan rinds in the freezer (worth doing!), drop one in while the stew simmers. It slowly melts into the broth adding a deep, savoury richness. Remove before serving.

Meal 2: Korean Braised Tofu with Zucchini & Mushrooms (Dubu Jorim)

Serves 4–5 | Prep: 10 min | Total: ~25 min | Serve over rice | Adjustable heat
▼

Crispy pan-fried tofu and vegetables braised in a sticky, savoury glaze with zucchini and mushrooms built right in. The glaze is made mild for the whole family — then adults add gochujang (Korean chili paste) at the table to their own taste. One cook, no separate baby portion needed.

Ingredients

For the tofu and vegetables:

  • 2 blocks (14 oz each) extra-firm tofu, pressed and cut into ½-inch slabs or rectangles
  • 2 medium zucchini, halved lengthways and sliced into half-moons
  • 8 oz cremini or shiitake mushrooms, sliced
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided
  • 3 green onions, sliced (white and green parts separated)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, minced
  • Sesame seeds, for garnish

For the base glaze (mild — baby-friendly):

  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar or maple syrup
  • 2 tablespoons water

For adult heat (at the table):

  • 2–3 tablespoons gochujang, served on the side
  • Sriracha as an alternative

To serve:

  • 2 cups dry white rice, cooked

Instructions

  1. Start the rice cooking. Mix the base glaze ingredients together in a small bowl (soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, brown sugar, water) and set aside. Put the gochujang in a small serving bowl at the table so adults can add heat to their own plates.
  2. Press tofu between paper towels for a few minutes to remove excess moisture, then cut into ½-inch thick rectangles or squares. Pat dry — dry tofu = crispier tofu.
  3. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a large non-stick skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add tofu in a single layer and cook without moving for 3–4 minutes until deeply golden and crispy on the bottom. Flip and cook another 2–3 minutes. Remove and set aside.
  4. Add remaining 1 tablespoon oil to the pan. Add mushrooms and cook 3–4 minutes without stirring until golden. Add zucchini and cook another 2–3 minutes until just tender. Add white parts of green onions, garlic, and ginger — stir-fry 1 minute until fragrant.
  5. Return tofu to the pan. Pour the mild base glaze over everything and toss gently to coat. Let it bubble and reduce for 1–2 minutes — it will thicken and cling to everything. Taste and season. Adults add gochujang at the table.
  6. Serve over rice, garnished with green onion tops and sesame seeds. Set gochujang and sriracha on the table for adults to add to taste.
🕐 Make-Ahead: Mix the base glaze up to 5 days ahead and keep in a jar in the fridge. Press and cut the tofu the night before and refrigerate. Day-of cooking is then just 20 minutes of pan work.
🍼 For baby (14 months+): Because the gochujang is served at the table rather than cooked in, the whole dish is already baby-friendly. The mild base glaze has a gentle savoury sweetness that works well for this age. Serve tofu, zucchini, and mushrooms cut into small pieces over rice — all three are great textures at 14 months. Just don't add gochujang to baby's bowl. High in protein and iron from the tofu.
Storage: Leftovers keep 3–4 days in the fridge — reheat in a skillet with a tiny splash of water to loosen the glaze, or microwave. The tofu loses its crispiness on reheating but still tastes great.
🌶️ Gochujang note: Gochujang is the key ingredient — it's a fermented Korean chili paste with a deep, complex heat that's quite different from sriracha. Most large supermarkets carry it in the international aisle, and it's always available at Asian grocery stores. It keeps in the fridge for months. Put a small bowl of it on the table and let adults dollop on as much as they like — 1 tablespoon per adult bowl is a good starting point.

👶 Baby Breakfast

Banana Oat Pancakes

Baby Breakfast | Makes ~16–20 small pancakes | Prep: 5 min | Cook: 15 min
▼

Simple 4-ingredient pancakes perfect for little ones. Naturally sweet, soft, and easy for small hands to grab. Freezes beautifully — make a big batch on the weekend for quick daycare breakfasts all week.

Ingredients

  • 2 ripe bananas
  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 2 eggs
  • ½ teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon butter or oil (for the pan)

Instructions

  1. Add bananas (broken into chunks), oats, eggs, and cinnamon to a blender or food processor. Blend until smooth, about 20–30 seconds. The batter will be thinner than traditional pancake batter — that's normal. Let it rest 5 minutes so the oats absorb liquid and the batter thickens slightly.
  2. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium-low heat. Add butter or oil and spread around. Medium-low is important — these brown faster than regular pancakes because of the banana sugars.
  3. Pour about 1 tablespoon of batter per pancake (silver-dollar size). Cook until bubbles form on the surface and edges look set, about 2 minutes.
  4. Gently flip each pancake and cook 1–2 more minutes until golden on the bottom. Use a thin spatula — these are delicate. Transfer to a plate or cooling rack.
  5. Cool completely before storing. For daycare: pack 3–4 in a small container. Great cold or microwaved for 20–30 seconds.
🍼 For baby (14 months+): These were designed for baby! Serve at room temperature — small enough to grab, soft enough to eat easily. At 14 months with good teeth these are a great texture. Add 1 tbsp peanut butter to the blender for extra protein and healthy fat. Also great with a small smear of yogurt on top.
Freezer Instructions: Cool completely, lay in a single layer on a parchment-lined sheet and freeze 1–2 hours, then transfer to a freezer bag. Keeps up to 3 months. Microwave straight from frozen for 30–45 seconds, or pull out the night before.
💡 Variations: Toss a small handful of spinach into the blender — it disappears in the batter and adds iron. For adults: top with yogurt and fresh berries.
