Week 1 Meal Plan

Vegetarian dinners with leftovers for lunch | 4-5 servings per meal

Monday: Sheet Pan Roasted Vegetable Fajitas

Serves 4-5 | Time: 35 minutes
▼

Roasted bell peppers, onions, and zucchini with cumin and smoked paprika, served with warm tortillas, black beans, cheese, and all the fixings.

Ingredients

  • 3 bell peppers (mixed colors), sliced into strips
  • 1 large onion, sliced into strips
  • 1 medium zucchini, sliced into half-moons
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1½ teaspoons cumin
  • 1½ teaspoons smoked paprika
  • ¾ teaspoon chili powder
  • ¾ teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cans (15 oz each) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 8-10 flour tortillas
  • 1½ cups shredded cheese (Mexican blend or cheddar)
  • Optional toppings: salsa, avocado, sour cream, cilantro, lime wedges

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line one or two large baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, toss the sliced peppers, onions, and zucchini with olive oil, cumin, smoked paprika, chili powder, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread vegetables evenly across the baking sheet(s). Roast for 25-30 minutes, stirring halfway through, until vegetables are tender and slightly charred.
  4. While vegetables roast, warm black beans in a pot on the stove with a pinch of cumin and salt.
  5. Warm tortillas in the oven (wrapped in foil) for the last 5 minutes of cooking, or individually in a dry skillet.
  6. Serve family-style: let everyone build their own fajitas with roasted vegetables, black beans, cheese, and toppings.
For baby: Set aside a portion of roasted vegetables and black beans before adding heavy seasoning. Chop vegetables into small, manageable pieces. Mash beans slightly. Offer small strips of soft tortilla.
Storage tip: Store roasted vegetables, beans, and toppings separately. Reheat vegetables in a skillet for best texture.

Tuesday: Creamy Coconut Red Lentil Curry

Serves 4-5 | Time: 30 minutes
▼

Red lentils simmered with coconut milk, tomatoes, ginger, garlic, and garam masala. Served over rice with naan or flatbread.

Ingredients

  • 1½ tablespoons olive oil or coconut oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1½ tablespoons fresh ginger, minced
  • 1½ tablespoons garam masala
  • ¾ teaspoon cumin
  • ¾ teaspoon turmeric
  • ¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, or to taste)
  • 1½ cups red lentils, rinsed
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 can (13.5 oz) coconut milk
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1½ teaspoons salt (or to taste)
  • 1½ cups spinach, roughly chopped
  • Juice of ½ lemon
  • Rice for serving (2 cups uncooked)
  • Naan or flatbread for serving

Instructions

  1. Start cooking rice according to package directions (2 cups uncooked rice).
  2. Heat oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add onion and cook for 5 minutes until softened.
  3. Add garlic and ginger, cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Add garam masala, cumin, turmeric, and cayenne. Stir for 30 seconds.
  5. Add red lentils, diced tomatoes (with juice), coconut milk, and vegetable broth. Stir well.
  6. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer uncovered for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until lentils are completely soft and broken down.
  7. Stir in spinach and cook until wilted, about 2 minutes.
  8. Add salt and lemon juice to taste.
  9. Serve over rice with naan on the side.
For baby: Before adding cayenne, remove a portion of the curry. The lentils should be very soft and easily mashed. Serve with rice and torn pieces of soft naan. You can thin with a bit of extra coconut milk if needed.
Storage tip: This curry actually improves overnight. Store curry and rice separately. Add a splash of water or broth when reheating as it thickens.

Wednesday: One-Pot Pasta Primavera

Serves 4-5 | Time: 25 minutes
▼

Pasta cooked directly in vegetable broth with cherry tomatoes, asparagus, peas, and spinach, finished with parmesan and lemon.

Ingredients

  • 12 oz pasta (penne, fusilli, or rotini)
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • 1½ cups water
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1½ cups cherry tomatoes, halved
  • ¾ lb asparagus, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1½ cups frozen peas
  • 2 cups fresh spinach
  • ¾ cup grated parmesan cheese, plus more for serving
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Fresh basil (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large, deep skillet or wide pot, combine pasta, vegetable broth, water, and garlic. Bring to a boil over high heat.
  2. Add cherry tomatoes and asparagus. Reduce heat to medium-high and cook, stirring frequently, for 10-12 minutes until pasta is nearly al dente and most liquid is absorbed.
  3. Add frozen peas and spinach. Stir until spinach is wilted and peas are heated through, about 2-3 minutes.
  4. Remove from heat. Stir in parmesan cheese, lemon zest, lemon juice, and olive oil. The residual heat will create a light, creamy sauce.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste. If too dry, add a splash of pasta cooking water or broth.
  6. Serve with additional parmesan and fresh basil if desired.
For baby: This is naturally baby-friendly! Set aside a portion before adding lemon juice and heavy seasoning. Cut pasta into small pieces, ensure vegetables are soft. The peas and cut asparagus tips are great for self-feeding practice.
Storage tip: This pasta can dry out, so store with a bit of extra liquid (broth or olive oil). Reheat gently with a splash of water.

Thursday: Black Bean & Sweet Potato Quesadillas

Serves 4-5 | Time: 30 minutes
▼

Roasted sweet potato cubes mashed with black beans and cheese, folded into quesadillas and pan-fried until crispy. Serve with salsa and avocado.

Ingredients

  • 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed (about ½-inch pieces)
  • 1½ tablespoons olive oil
  • ¾ teaspoon cumin
  • ¾ teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 2 cups shredded cheese (Mexican blend, cheddar, or monterey jack)
  • 8-10 flour tortillas (8-inch)
  • Oil or butter for cooking
  • Toppings: salsa, sour cream or Greek yogurt, avocado, cilantro

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Spread sweet potato cubes on a large baking sheet. Toss with olive oil, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  2. Roast for 20-25 minutes, stirring halfway through, until tender and starting to brown.
  3. While sweet potatoes roast, roughly mash black beans in a bowl (leave some texture).
  4. When sweet potatoes are done, mash them roughly with a fork or potato masher. Mix with the mashed black beans.
  5. Heat a large skillet or griddle over medium heat. Lightly brush with oil or butter.
  6. For each quesadilla: place a tortilla in the pan, spread about ⅓ cup of the bean-sweet potato mixture on half, sprinkle with cheese, fold in half. Cook 2-3 minutes per side until golden and cheese is melted.
  7. Repeat with remaining tortillas. You can keep finished quesadillas warm in a 200°F oven.
  8. Cut into wedges and serve with toppings.
For baby: The bean and sweet potato filling is perfect on its own - no tortilla needed. Offer a small portion with some mashed avocado. If baby is doing well with finger foods, you can offer small pieces of quesadilla cut appropriately.
Storage tip: Store filling and quesadillas separately if possible. Quesadillas reheat well in a skillet or oven (better than microwave for crispness).

Friday: Mediterranean Chickpea Bowls

Serves 4-5 | Time: 35 minutes
▼

Roasted chickpeas with za'atar, cucumber-tomato salad, hummus, couscous, and a lemon-tahini dressing.

Ingredients

For the chickpeas:

  • 2 cans (15 oz each) chickpeas, drained, rinsed, and patted dry
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1½ tablespoons za'atar (or 2 teaspoons dried oregano + 1 teaspoon sesame seeds)
  • ¾ teaspoon cumin
  • ¼ teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper

For the bowls:

  • 1½ cups couscous
  • 2 cups boiling water or vegetable broth
  • 1½ tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt
  • 1 cup hummus (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 large cucumber, diced
  • 2 cups cherry tomatoes, halved
  • ¼ red onion, finely diced
  • 3 tablespoons fresh parsley or cilantro, chopped
  • 1½ tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1½ tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

For the lemon-tahini dressing:

  • ⅓ cup tahini
  • 3 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 4-5 tablespoons water (to thin)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F.
  2. Make the chickpeas: Spread dried chickpeas on a baking sheet. Toss with olive oil, za'atar, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper. Roast for 25-30 minutes, shaking the pan halfway through, until golden and slightly crispy.
  3. Make the couscous: While chickpeas roast, place couscous in a large bowl. Add salt and olive oil. Pour boiling water or broth over couscous, stir, cover with a plate or plastic wrap, and let sit for 5 minutes. Fluff with a fork.
  4. Make the cucumber-tomato salad: In a bowl, combine cucumbers, tomatoes, red onion, and parsley. Dress with lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  5. Make the tahini dressing: Whisk together tahini, lemon juice, and garlic. Add water one tablespoon at a time until you reach a pourable consistency. Season with salt.
  6. Assemble bowls: For each serving, add a base of couscous, top with roasted chickpeas, cucumber-tomato salad, a dollop of hummus, and drizzle with tahini dressing.
For baby: Set aside chickpeas before adding heavy seasoning (or mash some of the roasted ones). Serve with couscous, small cucumber pieces (peeled and deseeded), and a small amount of hummus. The tahini dressing can be thinned and offered in small amounts.
Storage tip: Store all components separately. Chickpeas lose their crispness but are still delicious. Everything keeps well for 3-4 days.
