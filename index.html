Quick Weeknight Meals

Two easy dinners ready in 25 minutes or less | 4-5 servings each

Meal 1: Sheet Pan Gnocchi with Vegetables & Pesto

Serves 4-5 | Time: 25 minutes (mostly hands-off!)
▼

The ultimate easy weeknight dinner! Store-bought gnocchi gets crispy in the oven alongside roasted vegetables, then everything is tossed with pesto. One pan, minimal effort, maximum flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 packages (16 oz each) shelf-stable or refrigerated gnocchi
  • 2 cups cherry tomatoes
  • 1 medium zucchini, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, diced
  • 1 small red onion, diced
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • ½ teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • ½ cup basil pesto (store-bought or homemade)
  • ½ cup grated parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 450°F. Line a large baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. On the baking sheet, combine gnocchi, cherry tomatoes, zucchini, bell peppers, and red onion. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with garlic powder, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper. Toss everything together until well coated.
  3. Spread everything out in a single layer (don't overcrowd - use two pans if needed). Roast for 20-25 minutes, stirring halfway through, until gnocchi is golden and crispy and vegetables are tender.
  4. Remove from oven and immediately toss with pesto while everything is hot.
  5. Transfer to serving bowls and top with parmesan cheese and fresh basil if using. Serve hot!
For baby: Gnocchi is naturally soft and perfect for babies! Set aside a portion before adding pesto (or use just a tiny bit). Cut gnocchi and vegetables into appropriate sizes. The roasted veggies are soft and easy to pick up. Skip the parmesan if baby is under 12 months.
Storage tip: Leftovers keep for 3-4 days in the fridge. The gnocchi won't be as crispy when reheated, but it's still delicious! Reheat in a skillet over medium heat for best results, or microwave for convenience.
⚡ Speed tip: While the oven preheats, chop all your vegetables. Once they're on the pan, you're hands-free for 20 minutes!

Meal 2: Thai Peanut Noodles with Crispy Tofu

Serves 4-5 | Time: 20 minutes
▼

Silky rice noodles with crispy tofu and crunchy vegetables, all coated in a rich, creamy peanut sauce. Serve hot or cold - perfect for meal prep!

Ingredients

For the noodles and tofu:

  • 12 oz rice noodles (pad thai style)
  • 1 block (14 oz) extra-firm tofu, pressed and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided
  • 2 cups shredded red cabbage
  • 2 medium carrots, julienned or shredded
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup edamame (frozen, shelled)
  • 3 green onions, sliced
  • ¼ cup chopped peanuts
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Lime wedges for serving

For the peanut sauce:

  • ½ cup creamy peanut butter
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup or brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice (about 1 lime)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, minced
  • ¼ cup warm water (to thin)
  • 1 teaspoon sriracha or chili garlic sauce (optional)

Instructions

  1. Cook rice noodles according to package directions. Drain, rinse with cold water, and set aside.
  2. While noodles cook, make the peanut sauce: In a bowl, whisk together peanut butter, soy sauce, rice vinegar, maple syrup, lime juice, sesame oil, garlic, ginger, and warm water until smooth. Add sriracha if using. Set aside.
  3. Press tofu between paper towels to remove excess moisture, then cut into ½-inch cubes. Heat 1 tablespoon vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add tofu and cook 8-10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until golden and crispy on all sides. Remove and set aside.
  4. In the same skillet, add remaining 1 tablespoon oil. Add cabbage, carrots, and bell pepper. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until vegetables are tender-crisp. Add edamame and cook 1 more minute.
  5. Add cooked noodles, crispy tofu, and peanut sauce to the skillet. Toss everything together for 2-3 minutes until well coated and heated through. If the sauce is too thick, add a splash of water.
  6. Remove from heat. Divide among bowls and top with green onions, chopped peanuts, and cilantro. Serve with lime wedges. Can be served hot, room temperature, or cold!
For baby: Set aside plain noodles and vegetables before adding sauce. Cut tofu and noodles into small pieces. The soft noodles, edamame, and vegetables are great for self-feeding. You can add a tiny bit of the peanut sauce for flavor (check for peanut allergy first!), or serve plain. Skip whole peanuts for choking safety.
Storage tip: This is PERFECT for meal prep! Store in airtight containers for up to 5 days. The noodles will absorb some sauce, so you may want to thin it with a little water or extra soy sauce when reheating. Delicious served cold straight from the fridge!
🥡 Lunch hack: Make a double batch! This reheats beautifully or can be eaten cold for lunches all week. Pack with extra lime wedges and peanuts on the side.
