🍽️ Weeknight Dinners

Meal 1: West African Peanut Stew

Serves 4–5 | Time: 35–40 minutes | Serve over rice
▼

A deeply warming, pantry-friendly stew with bold flavors — sweet potato and chickpeas simmered in a rich tomato-peanut broth with warming spices. Comfort food at its most affordable. Inspired by the groundnut stews of West Africa.

Ingredients

For the stew:

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cut into ½-inch cubes
  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, minced (or 1 tsp ground ginger)
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • ½ cup creamy peanut butter
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 cups baby spinach
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ½ teaspoon coriander
  • Salt and pepper to taste

To serve:

  • 2 cups dry white rice (cooked according to package directions)
  • Lime wedges (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro (optional)
  • Chopped peanuts for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Start the rice cooking according to package directions — it'll be hands-off while you make the stew.
  2. Heat oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add onion and cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened and translucent.
  3. Add garlic and ginger. Cook 1 minute, stirring, until fragrant.
  4. Add tomato paste, cumin, smoked paprika, and coriander. Stir well and cook for 1 minute — this blooms the spices and deepens the flavor.
  5. Add sweet potato, chickpeas, diced tomatoes (with their liquid), and vegetable broth. Stir to combine. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to medium-low. Simmer uncovered for 15–20 minutes, until sweet potatoes are completely tender when pierced with a fork.
  6. Stir in peanut butter until fully dissolved into the broth. Simmer for 5 more minutes — the stew will thicken. If it gets too thick, add a splash more broth or water.
  7. Stir in spinach and cook 1–2 minutes until wilted. Taste and season generously with salt and pepper.
  8. Ladle over rice and finish with a squeeze of lime, cilantro, and chopped peanuts if desired. Serve hot!
🍼 For baby (12 months+): This stew is wonderful for baby! Before adding the peanut butter, scoop out a small portion of sweet potato and chickpeas. Mash or dice to an appropriate texture. The peanut butter stew itself is also fine for baby at this age — just let it cool and serve in small spoonfuls. Chickpeas can be offered whole (they're soft after simmering) or lightly squished. Skip or reduce the chili flakes if using. Great for self-feeding on a tray!
Storage & Freezer: Stew (without rice) keeps 4–5 days in the fridge and freezes beautifully for up to 3 months. Freeze in individual portions for easy weeknight reheat. The stew thickens when cold — add a splash of water or broth when reheating. Store rice separately.
⚡ Speed tip: Use pre-cubed frozen sweet potato from Costco or your grocery freezer section — throw it straight in, no peeling or chopping needed. Cuts prep to under 5 minutes!

Meal 2: Smoky Black Bean & Cheese Enchiladas

Serves 4–5 | Time: 40 minutes | Freezer-friendly
▼

Saucy, cheesy, deeply satisfying enchiladas with smoky cumin-spiced black beans. Mostly hands-off once they're in the oven, so you can set the table and get baby sorted while dinner finishes itself.

Ingredients

For the filling:

  • 2 cans (15 oz each) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ½ teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For assembly:

  • 8–10 flour tortillas (8-inch)
  • 1 can (28 oz) red enchilada sauce
  • 2 cups shredded Mexican cheese blend (or cheddar)

Optional toppings:

  • Sour cream
  • Avocado or guacamole
  • Fresh cilantro
  • Jalapeño slices
  • Lime wedges

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F.
  2. Heat oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add onion and cook 4–5 minutes until softened. Add garlic and cook 1 minute more.
  3. Add cumin, smoked paprika, and chili powder. Stir and cook 30 seconds until fragrant. Add black beans and stir to coat in the spices. Using the back of a spoon or a fork, mash roughly half the beans — this makes a sturdier filling that holds together in the tortilla. Cook 2–3 more minutes. Season with salt and pepper and remove from heat.
  4. Pour about ½ cup of enchilada sauce into the bottom of a 9×13-inch baking dish and spread to coat.
  5. Lay a tortilla flat. Spoon a heaping 3–4 tablespoons of bean filling down the center, then add a pinch of cheese (reserve most cheese for the top). Roll up tightly and place seam-side down in the dish. Repeat with remaining tortillas.
  6. Pour the remaining enchilada sauce evenly over the top of all the enchiladas. Sprinkle the remaining shredded cheese generously over everything.
  7. Cover tightly with foil and bake for 20 minutes. Remove foil and bake 5 more minutes until cheese is bubbly and starting to brown at the edges.
  8. Let rest 5 minutes before serving. Top with sour cream, avocado, and cilantro as desired.
🍼 For baby (12 months+): Enchiladas are great for baby! Cut a section of enchilada into small pieces — the tortilla softens in the sauce and is easy to eat. The beans are soft and high in iron. You can also offer plain cooked beans on the side. Skip spicy toppings like jalapeño but sour cream and avocado are excellent. A small strip of soft tortilla with mashed beans is perfect for self-feeding.
Storage & Freezer: Leftovers keep 4 days in the fridge — they actually get better as the tortillas absorb the sauce. To freeze: assemble the dish but don't bake. Cover tightly with foil and freeze up to 3 months. Bake from frozen at 375°F for 45–50 minutes (covered), then 10 minutes uncovered.
⚡ Speed tip: The bean filling takes 10 minutes. While it cools slightly, preheat the oven and set up your assembly line — sauce, beans, cheese, tortillas. Assembly goes fast once everything is laid out.

👶 Baby Snacks, Breakfasts & Lunches

Banana Oat Pancakes

Baby Breakfast | Makes ~16–20 small pancakes | Prep: 5 min | Cook: 15 min
▼

Simple 4-ingredient pancakes perfect for little ones. Naturally sweet, soft, and easy for small hands to grab. Freezes beautifully — make a big batch on the weekend for quick daycare breakfasts all week.

Ingredients

  • 2 ripe bananas
  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 2 eggs
  • ½ teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon butter or oil (for the pan)

Instructions

  1. Add the bananas (broken into chunks), oats, eggs, and cinnamon to a blender or food processor. Blend until smooth, about 20–30 seconds. The batter will be thinner than traditional pancake batter — that's normal. Let it rest 5 minutes so the oats absorb liquid and the batter thickens slightly.
  2. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium-low heat. Add a small amount of butter or oil and spread it around. Medium-low is key — these burn faster than regular pancakes because of the banana sugars.
  3. Pour about 1 tablespoon of batter per pancake onto the skillet (small silver-dollar size is perfect for little hands). Cook until bubbles form on the surface and the edges look set, about 2 minutes.
  4. Gently flip each pancake and cook for another 1–2 minutes until golden on the bottom. These are delicate, so a thin spatula helps. Transfer to a plate or cooling rack.
  5. Let pancakes cool completely before storing. For daycare, pack 3–4 pancakes in a small container — they're great cold or microwaved for 20–30 seconds.
🍼 For baby (12 months+): These pancakes were designed for baby! Serve at room temperature or slightly warm. Great for self-feeding — small enough to grab, soft enough to gum. You can add 1 tbsp of peanut butter to the blender for extra protein and healthy fat. Also excellent with a small smear of yogurt on top.
Freezer Instructions: Let pancakes cool completely. Lay in a single layer on a parchment-lined baking sheet and freeze 1–2 hours, then transfer to a freezer bag. Keeps up to 3 months. Pull out what you need the night before, or microwave straight from frozen for 30–45 seconds.
💡 Variations: Toss a small handful of spinach into the blender — it disappears in the batter and adds iron. For adults: top with yogurt and fresh berries.

Veggie-Loaded Cornbread Bites

Baby Snack / Lunch | Makes ~24 mini bites | Prep: 10 min | Bake: 15 min
▼

Savory, golden little bites packed with veggies and cheese. Perfect finger food for daycare lunches — sturdy enough to hold up in a lunchbox and easy for small hands to grab. Make a batch on Sunday and you're set for the week.

Ingredients

Dry:

  • ¾ cup cornmeal (medium grind)
  • ¾ cup all-purpose flour
  • 1½ teaspoons baking powder

Wet:

  • 2 eggs
  • ½ cup whole milk
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted (or oil)
  • ⅓ cup plain whole milk yogurt

Mix-ins:

  • ¾ cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • ½ cup frozen corn kernels, thawed
  • ½ red bell pepper, finely diced
  • ¼ teaspoon garlic powder
  • ¼ teaspoon smoked paprika (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a 24-cup mini muffin tin well with butter or oil, or use silicone mini muffin molds (easiest release).
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the cornmeal, flour, and baking powder.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk the eggs, then stir in milk, melted butter, and yogurt until smooth.
  4. Pour the wet mixture into the dry and stir until just combined — don't overmix, a few lumps are fine. Fold in the cheese, corn, bell pepper, garlic powder, and smoked paprika.
  5. Spoon batter into each mini muffin cup, filling about three-quarters full. A small cookie scoop works perfectly here.
  6. Bake for 12–15 minutes until tops are golden and a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. The edges should be just starting to pull away from the tin.
  7. Let cool in the tin 3–5 minutes, then turn out onto a cooling rack. For daycare, pack 4–6 bites in a container. Great at room temperature or microwaved for 15–20 seconds.
🍼 For baby (12 months+): These are sized perfectly for small hands as-is. The soft crumb and mild savory flavor make them a consistent win. Serve at room temperature. Pair with soft fruit, steamed veggies, or a protein like diced egg for a complete daycare lunch.
Freezer Instructions: Cool completely, then spread in a single layer on a parchment-lined baking sheet and freeze 1–2 hours. Transfer to a freezer bag — keeps up to 3 months. Microwave from frozen for 30–40 seconds.
💡 Variations: Swap bell pepper for finely diced zucchini or thawed frozen peas. For a Tex-Mex twist, add a pinch of cumin and use pepper jack cheese. Budget tip: cornmeal bought in bulk is extremely inexpensive — a large bag makes many batches.

Chickpea Nuggets

Baby Lunch | Makes ~20–24 nuggets | Prep: 10 min | Bake: 20 min
▼

Crispy on the outside, soft on the inside, and loaded with protein and iron. A perfect plant-powered finger food for little ones and a great daycare lunch that reheats well. Adults love these too with a dipping sauce.

Ingredients

  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • ½ cup breadcrumbs (plain or panko)
  • 1 tablespoon tahini
  • 1 egg
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • ½ teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ¼ teaspoon cumin
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley or cilantro, finely chopped (optional)
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (for brushing)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Add the drained chickpeas to a mixing bowl. Mash with a fork or potato masher until mostly broken down but still a bit chunky — keep some texture. Don't blend smooth; a few whole chickpeas are fine.
  3. Add breadcrumbs, tahini, egg, garlic powder, smoked paprika, cumin, parsley (if using), and a pinch of salt. Stir everything together until it forms a thick, scoopable mixture. If too wet, add another tablespoon of breadcrumbs. If too dry, add a tiny splash of water.
  4. Using about 1 tablespoon of mixture per nugget, shape into small patties or ovals and place on the baking sheet. Flatten slightly to about ½ inch thick so they cook evenly. Brush or lightly drizzle the tops with olive oil.
  5. Bake 10 minutes, then carefully flip each nugget. Bake another 8–10 minutes until both sides are golden and firm to the touch.
  6. Let cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes — they firm up more as they cool. For daycare, pack 4–5 nuggets in a container. Great on their own or with a small side of hummus for dipping.
🍼 For baby (12 months+): These are sized and textured perfectly for this age. The slightly chunky texture is great for developing chewing skills. Serve at room temperature — no sauce needed, but a little yogurt or hummus on the side is great for dipping practice. The chickpeas and tahini are excellent iron and protein sources.
Freezer Instructions: Cool completely. Freeze in a single layer on a parchment-lined sheet for 1–2 hours, then transfer to a freezer bag. Keeps up to 3 months. Reheat from frozen: microwave 45–60 seconds, or toaster oven for a crispier result.
💡 Variations: Swap tahini for peanut butter or sunflower seed butter. Mix in ¼ cup finely grated carrot or zucchini (squeeze out moisture first). For a cheesy version, add 2 tbsp grated parmesan. Swap smoked paprika for mild curry powder for a different profile.
