Sweet Potato & Chickpea Buddha Bowl

Light, fresh, and baby-friendly | 4-5 servings | 35 minutes

Sweet Potato & Chickpea Buddha Bowl

Serves 4-5 | Time: 35 minutes
▼

A colorful, nutritious bowl with roasted sweet potatoes, crispy chickpeas, fresh vegetables, and creamy tahini sauce. Naturally sweet and perfect for the whole family, including baby!

Ingredients

For the bowl:

  • 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed (½-inch pieces)
  • 2 cans (15 oz each) chickpeas, drained, rinsed, and patted dry
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups cooked quinoa or brown rice (about 1 cup uncooked)
  • 4 cups mixed greens (spinach, arugula, or spring mix)
  • 2 cucumbers, diced
  • 2 cups cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • ¼ cup sunflower seeds or pumpkin seeds (optional)

For the creamy tahini sauce:

  • ½ cup tahini
  • ¼ cup lemon juice (about 2 lemons)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 6-8 tablespoons water (to thin)
  • ½ teaspoon maple syrup or honey (optional)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Cook quinoa or rice according to package directions.
  2. On a large baking sheet, toss sweet potato cubes with 1½ tablespoons olive oil, half the cumin, half the paprika, half the garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. On a second baking sheet (or the other half of a large sheet), toss chickpeas with remaining 1½ tablespoons olive oil and remaining spices.
  4. Roast both for 25-30 minutes, stirring halfway through. Sweet potatoes should be tender and starting to caramelize; chickpeas should be golden and slightly crispy.
  5. While roasting, make the tahini sauce: Whisk together tahini, lemon juice, and garlic. Add water one tablespoon at a time until you reach a pourable, creamy consistency. Add maple syrup if using, and season with salt. Set aside.
  6. To assemble bowls: Start with a base of quinoa or rice, add a handful of mixed greens, then arrange roasted sweet potatoes, chickpeas, cucumbers, tomatoes, and avocado on top.
  7. Drizzle generously with tahini sauce and sprinkle with seeds if using.
For baby (10 months): This meal is PERFECT for baby! The roasted sweet potatoes are naturally sweet and soft. Before adding spices heavily, set aside some plain sweet potato cubes. Mash a few chickpeas (or leave whole if baby is comfortable). Offer small cucumber pieces (peeled and deseeded), and tiny pieces of avocado. The quinoa/rice is great finger food. Thin the tahini sauce with extra water and offer a small amount - babies often love it! Skip the seeds for choking safety.
Storage tip: Store components separately for best results. Roasted sweet potatoes and chickpeas keep for 4 days. Keep the sauce, greens, and fresh vegetables separate. Assemble bowls fresh when ready to eat. The sweet potatoes and chickpeas are delicious served warm or cold!
💡 Make-ahead tip: Roast the sweet potatoes and chickpeas on the weekend and store in the fridge. Throughout the week, you can quickly assemble fresh bowls in just 5 minutes!
